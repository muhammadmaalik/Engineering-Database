02
The mission

      

        
In the spirit of physicist Giuseppe Occhialini: reveal what others cannot see.

        
YOUR ENGINEERING
MEMORY, IN ORBIT.

        

          
Occhialini turns your own computers into persistent engineering infrastructure. It understands project manifests, hardware devices, firmware, CAD, datasets, simulation state, and every decision that brought your build to life.

          
No subscription. No remote memory. No proprietary design sent to an unknown server. The system runs locally, syncs privately, and learns from the work you choose to preserve.

11 Mission briefing

        
COMMON SIGNALS.

      

      

        
Does Occhialini send my data to the cloud?+
No. Inference, vault storage, chat history, and training data stay on your machines. Private remote access is designed around WireGuard.

        
What GPU do I need?+
The current defaults target an 8GB-class NVIDIA laptop GPU using partial CUDA offload for a quantized 32B model. Smaller models can run on more modest hardware; a larger home GPU improves speed.

        
Can it work completely offline?+
Yes. Once your model and dependencies are installed, local inference and the desktop vault do not need an internet connection.

        
Can I use my own GGUF model?+
Yes. The model manager and configuration support selecting local GGUF files served through llama.cpp.

        
What happens during a sync conflict?+
Occhialini does not silently discard either copy. Near-equal timestamp conflicts are preserved as timestamped conflict files for review.

        
How does Isaac Sim connect?+
A small Python bridge inside Isaac Sim exposes a versioned local TCP JSON-line protocol. Occhialini can inspect scenes, control simulation time, and set joint targets.
